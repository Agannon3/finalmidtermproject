Risk Factors

Smoking

Smoking is terrible for your heart.
Smoking puts stress on your heart by increasing your blood pressure and heart Rate.
It also wears down your blood vessels, stiffens your arteries, and can cause
irregular heart rhythms.

Family History

Heart isease is a genetically linked disease.
If you have an older family member with heart disease
Your odds of suffering from heart disease in the future
are significantly higher.

Hypertension

High blood pressure defined as hypertension refers to blood pressure
over 140/90
Doctors typically advise adults to keep their blood pressure below
120/80. Hypertension weakens the heart muscle and your
blood vessels. Many patients with hypertension suffer from both
a Heart Attack and Stroke at some point in their life.

Poor Diet/High BMI

Having a poor diet high in sugar, saturated fats, and red meat will
increase your cholesterol levels and risk of diabetes.
Diabetes accelerates the deterioration of your cardiovascular system.

Age

It is no secret that as you get older your body's organ systems begin to deteriorate.
Most Heart Attacks and Strokes occur in patients over the age 50.
